Spike's 2007 Video Game Awards Recap
Tags: Hardware Review


December 10,2007 - Here we are the end of 2007 and yet another year of Spike’s Video Game Awards has passed. With so many big titles up for contention this year, there really weren’t any clear winners going into the show. But at the end of it all, it was 2K’s BioShock walking away with the biggest win of the night.

Somebody hopefully either learned their lesson last year (or they got their ass fired) with the Sarah Silverman fiasco, but thankfully they kept Samuel L. Jackson to host this year’s awards. The celebrities this time around were better, with names including Stan “The Man” Lee, Matt Groening (he created a little series called The Simpsons, for those who don’t know), and Tia Carrere. Spike didn’t disappoint with the a-holes, however, throwing Dave Navarro, Matt Leinart, and The Hills’ Heidi and Spencer (we’ll be damned if Heidi knows anything cohesive about video games). The show was headlined by The Foo Fighters, Video Games Live, and comedians Patton Oswalt, Brian Posehn, and Ralphie May.

Bioshock was the big daddy of the night Spike TV's VGA's
As stated, BioShock walked away with The Big Cheese of 2007, scoring wins for Best Original Soundtrack, Best Xbox 360 Game, and Game of the Year. The Jerk of the Show Award went to developer Gamecock, who overtook the microphone from Ken Levine during what would have been his acceptance speech. There was really no need for that and the Big Daddy that walked up on stage should have shoved its fake drill into their heads. Call of Duty 4 and The Orange Box walked away proud, with the former scoring Best Military and Best Shooter and the latter nabbing Best PC Game and Breakthrough Technology for Portal (they should treat themselves to some German Chocolate cake). Rachet & Clank took home the away for Best PS3 Game, while Super Mario Galaxy won Best Wii and Best Adventure Game.

Somewhat surprisingly, Halo 3 didn’t walk away with much. Best Multiplayer was basically a given, but the interesting win was Most Addicting Game Fueled by (Mountain) Dew. Stop the presses! Wasn’t Halo 3 advertised like hell in Mountain Dew products? Didn’t they release a soda that rotted your teeth 100 times faster than any other soda? Why yes, we believe they did. Halo 3 failed to score any other awards, most likely sorrowed by the tears of rabid Halo fanboys across the nation.

EA scored big time for overall awards. The Simpsons Game won Best Game Based on a Movie or TV Show, Madden 08 won Best Team while Skate won Best Individual Sports Game. Those Orange Box wins also count.

While the dancing monkey men weren’t all that interesting, the show overall was a step up from last year. Obviously, sex sells, so it was no surprise Spike had painted naked women present the physical awards. The Foo Fighters were as electric as ever and Video Games Live was great, but the comedians weren’t at the top of their game. Sam Jackson’s performance was a step up from last year.

And of course, Spike filled out the show with world premiere trailers. The live action trailer for Rainbow Six 2 was cool, but the lack of any gameplay was a disappointment. Sam Jackson also premiered his own video game, based on his series, Afro Samurai. It didn’t show too much but was a tad bit cooler than the RS2 trailer. The Grand Turismo 5 trailer was just straight gorgeous to look at.

If you didn’t watch the show, no big deal. Hell, you may have known who won everything for the past few days. They have been online, negating the need to watch the show. Regardless, here are the rest of the night’s winners:

Best Rhythm Game
Rock Band

Best RPG
Mass Effect

Best Driving Game
DiRT

Studio of the Year
Harmonix

Best Handheld Game
The Legend of Zelda: The Phantom Hourglass

Best Soundtrack
Rock Band
