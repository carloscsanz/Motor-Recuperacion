The Estate Road Show is proud to showcase a special offering of Rare and Eclectic baseball memorabilia from the lifelong collection of noted baseball scholar and maven Dr. Roy Ryden Anderson. Dr. Andersons passion for and devotion to protecting the memory of the Greatest Game Ever Played has permeated his everyday life to the point that his choices are simple, to divest part of his mammoth ultra high quality collection, or build a museum! The Anderson sale will afford other dedicated collectors the opportunity to acquire all manner of outstanding quality baseball cards, books, gloves, bats, and special rare museum grade items. The collection is so large that the Estate Road Show will be featuring these great pieces from January 17 up through the start of the 2010 major league season. Not only is Dr. Anderson a respected baseball historian, but he has touched the lives of many of the finest legal minds in the country in his capacities as both Professor (awarded the Vinson & Elkins Distinguished Teaching Fellow) and Administrator at the Southern Methodist University Law School. 

Roy Ryden Anderson


Elkins Distinguished Teaching Fellow and Professor of Law SMU

B.A. 1966 Texas Christian University
J.D. 1969 Southern Methodist University
LL.M. 1975 Yale University

           Professor Anderson is the Senior Associate Dean for Academic Affairs at the Southern Methodist University in Dallas, Tx. A former notes and comments editor of the Journal of Air Law and Commerce,Professor Anderson has served at SMU School of Law as executive director of the criminal justice program and as assistant dean and associate dean.  He teaches in the areas of contracts, commercial law, and damages.  He served for many years as the adviser for the SMU Law Review and for the moot court program. He is the author of numerous law journal articles, a two-volume treatise entitled Damages Under the Uniform Commercial Code (2d ed. 2003), and co-author of Anderson, Bartlett & Easts Texas Uniform Commercial Code Annotated (2002).  He is also co-author of the three volumes of theTexas Litigation Guide dealing with commercial litigation. Professor Anderson is a member of the State Bar of Texas, the American Bar Association, the American Law Institute and a life fellow of the Texas Bar Foundation.  From 1990-2000 he was a commentator for the ABA Task Force of advisors to the UCC Article 2 Drafting Committee and a member of the ALI Consultative Group for the UCC Article 2 Drafting Committee.

Professor Andersons recent article, The Wolf at the Campfire: Understanding Confidential Relationships, won the Texas Bar Foundations award for the outstanding law review article for 2000.

 Professor Anderson regularly teaches Contracts I & II, Sales, and Commercial Remedies.
