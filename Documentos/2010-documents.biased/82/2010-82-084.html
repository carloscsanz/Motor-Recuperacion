Yahoo! Weekly News January 9, 2009

Yahoo! Gains Traction With TV Widgets
(Extracted from San Francisco Chronicle, January 7, 2009) Yahoo! came in with the intent of showing off its Yahoo! Widget Channel and so far, some of the biggest TV makers are playing ball. Samsung, Toshiba and LG announced they will be installing the Yahoo! Widgets on select connected TVs. The services will be branded under different names but they deliver Yahoo!'s basic service of news, information and entertainment from the web directly on the TV. Users can pull down videos and news from Yahoo! or check stock quotes and pull pictures from Flickr. The widgets will also incorporate third-party content, not just Yahoo! properties. Users will be able to pull it up off a menu along the bottom of their screen. Patrick Barry, vice president of Yahoo!, made an appearance Wednesday at the Samsung press conference and talked about Yahoo!'s goals of creating a cinematic Internet experience for TV watchers. "This is about providing the best of the web for the TV context," he said.

Yahoo! Bringing Internet To Samsung Television Sets
(Extracted from Agence France Presse, January 7, 2009) Yahoo! and Samsung on Monday said they will put the California firm's software to work linking the South Korean electronics giant's televisions to the Internet. New Samsung high-density flat-panel television models to be displayed this week at a premier Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas will feature Yahoo! "widgets," mini applications which access Internet services. Samsung televisions built with an "Internet@TV" content service will let viewers check online news or stock portfolios as well as see videos or pictures on the Web, according to the companies. "Through this partnership, we can combine the Internet benefits of user choice and personalization with Samsung's leading product innovation and global reach to deliver a new experience to users around the world," said Yahoo!'s Connected TV vice president Patrick Barry.

Yahoo! Nears End of Search for a CEO
(Extracted from Wall Street Journal, January 9, 2009) Yahoo! Inc. is in the final stretch of its search for a chief executive officer, and a decision could come as soon as next week, said people close to the Internet company. Among candidates still under consideration is Carol Bartz, the former chief executive officer of Autodesk Inc., a publicly traded company that builds design software used in engineering. She's "on the list," said a person familiar with the matter, adding that she has impressed the Yahoo! directors she has met so far. A Yahoo! spokesman declined to comment. An Autodesk spokeswoman said Ms. Bartz was traveling Thursday and couldn't be reached for comment.

Sources: Yahoo!'s Decker A Strong Contender For CEO
(Extracted from CNET News.com, January 8, 2009) Yahoo! may have fallen out of favor with Wall Street after the failed Microsoft buyout bid, but that doesn't mean you can count Yahoo! President Sue Decker out of the running for CEO, people familiar with executive search say. Decker has undergone two full rounds of in-depth interviews with Yahoo!'s board, according to sources. And, in executive recruiting circles, more than one in-depth interview with an internal candidate signals a strong contender, said David Nosal, CEO of executive recruiting firm Nosal Partners.

Microsoft CEO: Time Is Right For Yahoo! Search Deal
(Extracted from Dow Jones, January 9, 2009) Microsoft Corp. Chief Executive Steve Ballmer said a search deal with Yahoo! Inc. should "probably be made" during the current management transition at both companies, the Financial Times reported Thursday. "We now have someone in place running our online business, and Yahoo!'s out looking for a CEO," Ballmer told the paper in an interview. "If a search deal is to be made, it's probably to be made in the interim period for new leaders in both places."

Report Of Microsoft Backing Bid For Yahoo! Is Disputed
(Extracted from the Seattle Post-Intelligencer, January 8, 2009) Microsoft Corp. isn't holding talks to finance a new bid for Yahoo! Inc., a person familiar with Microsoft's plans said Wednesday, responding to a blog report. TechCrunch said Wednesday that an investment group led by Silicon Valley executives and bankers is putting together a bid for Yahoo! that would be funded largely by Microsoft. The software company would also acquire Yahoo!'s Internet-search business under the deal, the blog said. The report revived speculation that Microsoft would make another play for Yahoo!, following a failed takeover attempt last year. Microsoft isn't discussing such a plan, said the person, who declined to be identified because the information is confidential. The deal was characterized as a proposal to Microsoft, TechCrunch said.

Yahoo!'s Tough Year May Not Be Over
(Extracted from New York Times, January 5, 2009) It's no secret that 2008 was a tough year for Yahoo!. There were the failed merger negotiations with Microsoft and the failed advertising partnership with Google. For many employees, much of the pain came from two rounds of layoffs, seemingly endless rounds of reorganizations and a sense that they were riding on a rudderless ship. Clearly Yahoo! had hoped for better things when the year began. In the first week of January 2008, Jerry Yang, right, a Yahoo! founder, gave his first major public speech as chief executive at the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas. There, Mr. Yang gave attendees a peek at one of the company's high-profile product efforts: a version of its popular Yahoo! Mail that was rebuilt into a powerful communications hub. It would allow users to access third-party applications that would be tightly integrated with e-mail, and it would reorganize inboxes into a social platform that could, for instance, prioritize messages based on a user's preferences.
