Lenovo Y460 Review

ok, so ive finally transfered most of my stuff over to my new lenovo y460.

Also, ive cleaned up whatever bloatware shit that are usually included in oem machines like anti viruses, power management shait and other funny stuff like cd burning software etc etc..

i also reinstalled some drivers because they were screwed. the video adapter drivers were especially screwed. at first it showed 4 gpus(see pic below)



this is probably due to some retarded registry error when they copied the factory image over to the laptop hard disk.

any computer retard who received a laptop like this would probably go WOO! IM GOOD! I HAVE 4 GRAPHIC ADAPTERS! GREAT LAPTOP!



i also found out 2 very depressing facts:

1) for now, there is NO WAY i can overclock my cpu T.T
2) for now, there is NO WAY i can overclock my GPU T.T


this is probably due to the hardware and model being new(launched in january) and all the enthusiast ppl havent created a program compatible with the hardware yet. OH NOES!


but overall, gaming performance at the weird ass native resolution(1366x768) is not bad. 40 frames avg in battlefield bad company 2 and about 80 fps in l4d and l4d 2 all maxed out with 2xaa and 2xaf




Specs
intel core i5-540m 2.53ghz turbo to 3.06ghz
4gb ddr3 1066mhz ram
500gb (I THINK ITS 5400rpm hdd)
14.1inch led screen
6 cell battery(with only like 4 hours battery life on integrated gfx-.-)
Ati mobility 5650 1gb gfx card
intel 1000 wifi card
lame as hell jbl speakers

design:

ok so lenovo isnt known for its kick ass laptop designs but this one is quite different from the usual thinkpad range(maybe thats why its in the ideapad range >.> im not really familiar with lenovo designs cos the specs usually turn me off). its not exactly sleek and shiny but its not bulky and doesnt scream " im an old man using a bizness laptop" either


Display:
okay, so this is like my first laptop with an LED display. however, it is obvious that the LED screen is relatively low quality cos the colors and image quality is not very good.

the whites on the screen has the color of a white fluorescent lamps which is quite eye straining. the reds are overly rich and overall i feel there is a bluish tint throughout the entire screen which is quite normal for a low end screen.

with the screen being so small at 14.1 inches and having the pixel density of 1366x768, i expected the pixels to be relatively unnoticeable but it seems like i was wrong. when reading words/fonts, pixels are really obvious(for me at least) when the alphabet has curves(eg B or C or G). you can see pixel edges on the alphabets. its kind of hard to describe but anyone who knows me well would know that i am 'pixel sensitive' and can spot pixels very easily. and yes, they annoy the hell out of me.


heat issues and air circulation:

needless to say, i used the laptop in bed for 2 nights to test out how well the cooling solution is. the cooling solution is basically, 1 fucking fan on the gpu and a heatsink pipe on the cpu. yes i opened it up(oops, shldnt have said that).

because one of the free gifts is a china shit cooling pad with fancy homo lights, the temps with the cooling pad on wasnt exactly that bad. id still say the temps arent good though. with the cooling pad, gaming for about half an hour brings the temps to a peak of 83 degrees for battlefield and l4d2. on less demanding games like street fighter 4, it peaks at about 75.

now i havent tried it without the cooling pad but i have a hunch it WILL hit 90 with or without a cooling pad if i play for more than an hour. which means i probably have to open it up and put my own thermal paste on the cpu and gpu.

the fan is really pathetic. i would have expected the fan to make at least some sound when maxed out. yes its totally silent and you dont even know theres a fan present until you feel SOME air coming out of the vent at the left side when the keyboard is burning up.

yes, the heat travels round the entire laptop and the keyboard, especially the area at the WSAD keys(where the gpu is and where heat sink pipe leads to).

having it on your lap will probably sterilize you or in some cases, make fried bird out of your penis.


sound quality:

EWWWWWWWWWW! compared to my alienware and previous xps 1730, this laptop's speakers redefine the word FAIL. on the marketing level, they claim to have dolby home theatre certification and JBL speakers. but in reality, it sucks so badly i think if i fart, the sound created will be deeper and clearer than what the speakers produce.

they seem to have a lack of output channels and lack of audio frequency range.

playing a simple mp3 song like

the thespian by alesana




gives such an awful output. the speakers dont seem to be capable of playing both the vocals and drums+background instruments at the same time. when there are only vocals and no drums/others, the sound quality seems NORMAL but the moment some other instrument plays, the vocals and instruments are sort of jumbled and extremely unclear. its just like blasting the same song on a really cheapo phone with 1 speaker with stereo widening on. all you hear is some trash bang bang bang and nothing else.

im not sure if its due to the stupid realtek audio driver or the audio chip or the damned speakers but all i know is that audio quality sucks bad

i also dislike the audio ports being static. on some laptops, you can change the microphone jack into an audio output jack to be able to have 2 speaker output jacks at the same time to achieve a quad speaker configuration at least. this however, is fixed and cannot be changed.


ports and others:

it has 4 usb 2.0 ports which is not bad for a 14 inch laptop, also a pcimia card and a dvd rw drive and hdmi+vga ports with touch sensitive buttons for the lulz. it lacks a memory card reader though

EDIT: I FOUND THE CARD READER LOL! it was beside the wireless switch all along.. damn you sneaky bastard..

vents at the bottom are all made of prasteek and feels very flimsy. hope it doesnt melt or anything -.-


synthetic benchmarks:





despite not being to overclock it, i feel that the laptop pumps out really decent scores. an average laptop with a decent graphics card would score about 4k in 3dmark06 and about 1-2k in vantage. this however, packs alot more punch that its peers of the same size.

these scores pwn an overclocked alienware M11x's scores badly according to the benchmark site here http://forum.notebookreview.com/showthread.php?t=459834

the highest overclocked score for 3dmark06 on the m11x is 7105 and the highest overclocked vantage score is 3278

mine are in the screenshots above so it shows that the y460's cpu and gpu are at least 10% better than the m11x's.

of course the m11x is smaller so it has a better selling point. however, since ive been carrying a 6-7kg laptop everyday to school, this y460 feels like a feather.

in fact, the adapter plus the laptop weighs just as much as MY ALIENWARE'S ADAPTER ONLY.









all in all, i give it a 7/10. great price at 1700 SGD(rounded off, could have been better but oh wel) with mid ranged specs. only flaw is the audio quality and mildy irritating screen.

Thursday, February 11, 2010

i am all that you fear, have i made myself clear?

had some benching urge today so i tried to overclock my ati cards for the very first time!

ati cards are a hell lot different in terms of display control tweaking and especially overclocking.

with the nvidia cards, all i had to do was open the control panel and drag the clock speeds to what i wanted.

these ati cards however, required the card bios to be flashed so its alot more troublesome but its alot more stable.

i had to create a bootable usb stick/thumbdrive with the hp flash drive thingy and then format the thumbdrive to be a dos bootable stick using windows 98 system files.

then i had to boot with the usb, type some commands to backup the current card bios, boot back to windows, use a program to hack/modify the bios, save it and then reboot using usb stick and flash the cards with the modified bios.


overclocked cpu to 3.28ghz and cards from 550 core 888 memory to 625 core 1025 memory. ati cards do not have shader cores :(






achieved higher scores than the nvidia ones which is expected given that they ARE supposed to be at least 10% faster. pretty impressed with the heat management as well. max temp 70 degrees and constant 58-65 range under load

only thing that sucks is no physx which makes games that cannot let you disable physx, like darkest of days and section 8 etc, unplayable. with the nvidia cards i had 80-90 fps in darkest of days with all maxed out and physx high.

with these atis, physx can only be put on low and cannot be off so i only get 25fps-.- same with section 8 i get 19fps when i usually get above 100
