Machine learning approaches for natural language processing : 6.891

http://www.ai.mit.edu/courses/6.891-nlp/

This website was created for a course by Michael Collins at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). The course covers "a survey of statistical or machine learning approaches to natural language processing with an emphasis on research issues". Lecture slides are provided in PostScript and PDF formats. These include the application of techniques such as hidden Markov models, stochastic context-free grammars, boosting and maximum-entropy methods, kernel methods over natural language structures, and learning from unlabelled or partially labelled data to statistical parsing and tagging problems, information extraction, machine translation, dialogue systems, and other natural language processing problems.
