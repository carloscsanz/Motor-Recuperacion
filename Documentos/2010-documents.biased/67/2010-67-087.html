    One of two things.

    Heat. Make sure your CPU heatsink is seated properly and use a program like CoreTemp to get an idea of what's going on thermally.

    Biostar is POS as far as motherboards go. They are inexpensive, but I've never heard anything good about them. You couldn't pay me to build a system around a Biostar mobo. (Actually you "could" pay me, but I'd laugh all the way to the bank on how you wasted your money )

    If you're not overclocking at all, I'd start swapping out components till you see what fails. If you ARE overclocking, go back to stock and see if you crash. If not, that's your problem, if you do, then test other components. I'd still put money on your mobo though.

    I know what it is you have the 512 MB 8800 GTS card ... Which is G92 based JUST like me....

    Well i am not sure if they programmed this video card wrong or something .. well the driver .. but the AUTO fan speed setting BLOWS. Mine did this its a heat issue on the video card.


    GO to ur nvidia control panel. Go to Performance, Adjust GPU settings...

    Set the speed to manual ... u will have to do this everytime you boot ur computer because it re-defaults to auto ... which auto SUCKS...

    i keep mine around 65% fan speed all the time ... EXCEPT when i am in a game i put it about 95%. Its not good to keep at 95% when u r just idleing.... but while playin games it needs it.

    Then no more issues. Your card will work just fine and not be overheating anymore. Cuz mine was around 70 celcius idle and 80+ under load. After doing this the card stays around 40-47 celcius under load or idle.
