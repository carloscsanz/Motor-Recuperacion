New Luggage Restrictions Encourage Us All to Travel Light

I don't usually check more than one bag when I travel - in fact, I don't check my bag at all, if I can help it! But now that most U.S. airlines are charging you $25 for your second bag, travelling light is more important than ever.

Though it may seem challenging at first, I have to say that travelling light is actually very liberating - plus, it's better for the environment, as a lighter plane (or car, for that matter) burns less fuel. Here are a few articles and sites I came across in my search for some good, practical tips on how to pack light:

  • Via one of my fave travel tip blogs, Girls Getaway, I found a site called One Bag, which offers fantastic (and scientific) tips on making the most of one bag when travelling. Very informative and helpful!

  • Carrying off the art of one carry-on by John Flinn at the San Francisco Chronicle is full of practical points and specific suggestions for eliminating the extra stuff you pack "just in case" and then never use. I particularly enjoyed his take on wrinkle-free clothes: "The catalogs are full of wrinkle-free travel clothes, but you can skip these if you've spent your adult life, as I have, carefully cultivating a rumpled look."

  • IndependentTraveler.com has a handy Interactive Packing List you can use while preparing for your next trip. I love this thing. Obviously, you won't need everything on the list - for example, I rarely need malaria tablets when I travel, but I DO often forget my swimsuit...

Naturally, because we carry not only laptop bags, but also stylish women's luggage at CareerBags.com, I have a few suggestions for which one bag you should take next time you fly. These bags all fit the current size regulations for carry-on luggage (of course, it's up to you to make sure they don't go over the weight restriction - maybe leave your rock collection at home).

Baggallini Daytripper Rolling Tote - This bag is one of the lightest carry-on bags out there - depending on what you pack in it, you can put it under your seat OR in the overhead compartment. The Daytripper has three separate interior compartments (including a padded one for your laptop) to help you keep things separate for easy access.

It also comes with a clear, one quart plastic pouch for all of your allowable liquids, which I find particularly cool - no more frantically stuffing your tiny hand lotion and collection of lipsticks into a Ziploc while in line for security.

Casauri Wheeled Mini Carry-on - The Casauri is light and roomy - perfect for a weekend or a week. Its exterior pocket allows for easy access to travel documents or your in-flight reading material (a must for me when I travel - the movies on the plane are always terrible when I fly. Why? Why is that??).

The Casauri comes in a variety of distinctive colours (handy if you decide to check in instead of carrying it on). And it's water-resistant with a protective bottom splash guard, in case you encounter a puddle (or a spilled latte) you just can't dodge.

If rolling bags aren't your cup of tea, I would suggest Baggallini's Hampton Bag as a great carry-on duffle. It's light as air and full of handy pockets, both open and zippered. It fits nicely over your shoulder and - let's face it - looks fantastic. I love this summer green colour, but it also comes in zebra and giraffe prints!

All this talk of travel has me fantasizing about my own summer vacation. Where shall I go? Europe? Thailand? Hawaii? Ah...fantasy is fun. I'm so busy this summer, I think I might have to vacation somewhere closer to home. Maybe a picnic in the backyard!

Tips for Choosing the Right Laptop Bag or Briefcase

As a woman who travels all over for work, I know first hand how hard it is to find the ideal business case.

You want a laptop bag or business case that holds everything from your laptop to your lip gloss. It has to withstand the daily abuse of travel to and from the airport, conferences, and business meetings. And of course, you want your bag to help complete your outfit and represent who you are as a woman (yes ladies, these last two are important).

Finding a functional, fashionable laptop bag to meet all of your business, travel and style needs can be a bit daunting. So I thought I'd jot down a few helpful tips about what we look for in our products at CareerBags.com.

Dimensions. Vendors measure their bags in two ways - "Length, width, depth" or "diagonally". If the details say that the bag will fit a 15" laptop, that doesn’t mean the width is 15". They get the 15" by measuring the diagonal distance from one corner to the other. So a 10" x 13" x 4" bag might fit your 15" laptop. Just be sure to measure your laptop according to the information provided.

If you're ordering online and you're still not sure if a bag will fit your laptop, be sure to call or email the store and ask about your bag first.

Weight. If you are a frequent traveller, you might want to look for something that is on wheels but that can also be detached so you don’t have to wheel your bag into a meeting. The Glen Ellyn 2-in-1 Rolling Tote is one of our best sellers and it's perfect for the woman business traveller. With its patented design, this bag looks great on or off wheels.

Pockets. From your laptop to your pens, everything should be organized and secure for easy access. You don't want to be fumbling through your bag looking for your business card when you’re trying to make a first impression. And if you're going through security a lot, it's really important to have a separate space in the bag just for your laptop. That way you can slip your computer out easily and not have to rearrange the contents of your bag to get it back in while you're also trying to put your shoes back on. The Coakley Everyday Portfolio is a great example of a functional, travel-friendly bag. This bag has more pockets then you'll know what to do with and includes a handy zip open pocket on the back that lets you slide it over the handle of your rolling luggage.

Price. Cost is obviously an important factor when choosing your laptop bag or business case. This is why at CareerBags.com we focus more on independent, woman-owned vendors rather than couture brand names. That way, we can give you a wide range of prices to choose from (while supporting our fellow business women!)

Style. Function is important, but so is style - your personal style. The right business bag should boost your confidence and help represent who you are, on the street and in the boardroom. Below are a few examples of how diverse business bags can be. There is something out there for everyone, whether you're classic, casual or funky/chic.
