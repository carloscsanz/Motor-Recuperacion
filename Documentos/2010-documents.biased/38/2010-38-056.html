Paul Donovan
Chief Executive
eircom

Paul Donovan was appointed a director and as Chief Executive Officer of eircom on 1 July 2009. Mr Donovan has significant telecom management and senior leadership experience from several markets in the world, including Asia Pacific and Africa. As of 1 July 2009 Mr Donovan is CEO of Eircom and prior to this he was Chief Executive, EMAPA Region for the Vodafone Group. Mr Donovan's background includes a decade in the fast moving consumer goods industry, before he moved into the technology sector, principally with BT and Vodafone. His career with Vodafone started 10 years ago and for the last five years, he has overseen Vodafone's operations in subsidiaries in Eastern Europe, Middle East and Asia Pacific. Africa, the US, India and China were added to his remit in 2006. As part of his role, he sat on the boards of numerous subsidiaries and joint ventures during this period. He holds a BA (with Honors) from University College, London and an MBA from the Bradford University Management Centre with a specialisation in Finance and Business Policy. He is presently on the boards of eircom Group Limited, eircom Limited and Valentia Telecommunications and was elected as a non-executive director of Millicom International Cellular SA in May 2009.
Tom Bambury
General Manager
ESB Telecoms

ESB Telecoms Ltd is a wholly owned subsidiary of ESB whose mission is to leverage the parent company’s assets into the telecommunications industry in Ireland. Tom Bambury has worked for the company since 2003 and during that time has held several key management roles. He was appointed General Manager of the company in February 2008.

Prior to ESB Telecoms, Tom lived in the USA where he gained extensive experience in the telecommunications industry and in particular in the then booming optical networking field, working for notable industry leaders Nortel Networks and Corning.

He graduated from UCC with a degree in electrical engineering and also holds a post-graduate degree in microelectronics as well as an MBA.
Conal Henry
Chief Executive
e-net

Conal Henry was appointed as CEO of e-net in April 2006. Conal previously held senior positions in a number of large and successful organisations including Ryanair, Procter & Gamble, Asda Stores and First Active. He has a degree in law from QUB and is a Chartered Management Accountant.
Robert Dunn
Chief Executive

UPC Ireland (new parent company of ntl & chorus)

Robert joined the Irish operation as CEO in April 2006. Robert is responsible for the operational management of both UPC’s residential and business divisions in Ireland. Under his leadership, two former cable companies have evolved to form one new company under the UPC brand, while at the same time creating a national communications and entertainment company to deliver a new kind of choice into the Irish marketplace.

Robert has worked at the European Head Office of UPC since May 2000, acting as Chief Financial Officer of the UPC Broadband Division. In this position, he has held responsibility for the financial control for the Division in Europe.

Prior to joining the company, Dunn served as Group Controller for Impress Metal Packaging in Amsterdam. Robert qualified as a Chartered Accountant with Price Waterhouse in London where he worked for nine years

Tim Fritzley
Chief Executive Officer

Intune Networks
Tim has been at the forefront of many new and innovative networking and service developments over his career while working at GTE, Tellabs, and most recently as the VP of Global Sales and Solutions at Microsoft TV. Tim successfully created and launched the IPTVE program, ecosystem, and partners to carriers around the world. Tim’s experience in network operations, network equipment development, and IPTV service platform creation and deployment gives him a broad and detailed perspective on the problems and issues the carriers are still facing in deploying and operating scaled IP networks for the delivery of converged voice, video and data services.

Chris Clark
Chief Executive
BT Ireland

Chris Clark was appointed CEO, BT Ireland in July 2007. Prior to taking up the role, Chris was Director Business Transformation for BT Retail where he managed a radical 2 year transformation programme making BT Retail: Better, simpler, faster and cheaper for its customers and its people.

Prior to that, Chris was CEO of Converged Mobility Operations for BT Retail. In this role, Chris led the commercial and operational performance across BT’s mobility division. This included managing BT Openzone and a wider responsibility for driving both business and consumer take-up of wi-fi for BT Group. Chris was also BT’s representative for the Wireless Broadband Association (WBA) where he was involved in driving European membership and helping turn the WBA into a leading global wi-fi alliance.

From October 1999 to April 2004 Chris was at the heart of BT’s international operations. Chris was President of BT Global Services’ Wholesale Business, a global business unit responsible for delivering in excess of €1.5 billion in revenues, with over 350 people in 16 countries. This business was created by the bringing together of BT’s former European joint ventures and Concert, BT’s international joint venture with AT&T. Chris led this business from its formation in April 2002 and prior to this Chris was CEO of Farland BV, BT’s pan European network company.

From 1996 to 1999 Chris was based in the US where several key business development roles culminated in his position as advisor to BT Worldwide’s President and CEO. Chris joined BT from university in 1991, and held a number of sales and product development roles before taking up posts in the US.
Robert Finnegan
Chief Executive

3

From Dunmore East in Waterford, a qualifed accountant, Robert Finnegan has been serving as Chief Executive of 3 in Ireland since December 2005.
An veteran of Hutchison Whampoa, one of the largest companies on the Hong Kong Stock exchance, Robert served as CEO of its beverage division – Watson’s – a 3,000-employee company with an annual turnover of US$200m. In this position, Finnegan strategically led the development of its water business in Europe under the Powwow brand, building it to market leader position. He also initiated and led the divestiture of Powwow to Nestle for US$545m. Prior to his role as CEO at Watson’s, Finnegan held positions as Financial Controller and Finance and Strategic Planning Director at Hutchison for six years.

Robert has also held senior finance roles at Lyons Tetley and Procter and Gamble.

Danuta Gray
Chief Executive
Telefónica O2 Ireland

Danuta Gray is Chief Executive of Telefónica O2 Ireland, a position she has held since 2001. During this period Danuta has spearheaded the company’s growth from a customer base of 1 million to its highest ever number of customers at 1.7 million, making Telefónica O2 Ireland the second largest mobile communications company in the Irish market. Following the acquisition of O2 by Spanish operator Telefónica in February 2006, Danuta was appointed to the Telefónica O2 Europe plc board.

She is a board member of Irish Life & Permanent plc and in August 2006 was appointed non-executive Director of Aer Lingus by the then Minister for Transport, Martin Cullen T.D. Danuta is also Chairperson of the board of children’s charity Barretstown Gang Camp.

With almost 20 years experience in the mobile industry, Danuta joined Telefónica O2 Ireland from BT Europe in Germany, where she held the position of Director and was responsible for managing BT’s investment in VIAG Interkom (now Telefónica O2 Germany). Prior to this Danuta was General Manager at BT Mobile in the UK. Danuta has also worked abroad on assignments in the USA, Singapore and Switzerland.

Danuta holds a BSc in BioPhysics from the University of Leeds and also has an MBA. She is married to Andrew and they have two sons.
Charles Butterworth
Chief Executive
Vodafone Ireland

In July 2007 Charles Butterworth took up the position as CEO of Vodafone Ireland, succeeding Teresa Elder. He joined Vodafone Group as the director of group corporate finance in May 2002 where he managed and co-ordinated merger and acquisition activity. In 2004 he was appointed as group investor relations director. In this role he was responsible for orchestrating an active dialogue with investors and analysts, covering all aspects of Vodafone's business including operational strategy and financial performance.

Before joining Vodafone Group, Butterworth spent nine years with UBS investment bank where he specialised in the telecoms sector, and in particular mobile. He is a board member of Vodafone Italy.
