Flashback - Windows XP

October 25th, 2006

Windows XP is now 5 years old! Five years ago, on 25th of October 2001, Microsoft released Windows XP. This OS was installed on millions and millions of computers in the whole world, today reaching the incredible figure of 88,60% of all PC’s. Known as codename “Whistler” the launch took place 3 days before Bill Gate’s birthday. He will be 51 years old the day after tommorrow.

Over the years, Whistler was the target of numerous hacking attempts, exploits and bugs but Microsoft successfully filled in the security holes and released patches. Therefore, Service Pack 1 was officially launched on September 9 2002, at less than one year from XP’s official release. Since the number of attacks continued to grow and since the uPNP was actually a great security hole, they’ve also released Service Pack 2 on August 25 2004 that was meant to correct all of the bugs in the original installation aswell as all of the discovered security breaches.

A year and a few months ago, when I was working for Softpedia (software news editor), a so called package appeared on the net, that contained not more nor less than 171 patches. The compilation was called Service Pack 3, and I had the job to test it. I decided to test it on my home PC, and that was a big mistake. After spending about two hours installing the 171 updates by hand, after the reboot, my system was working very very S-L-O-W so I had to remove it. I have just found out that Microsoft has officially delayed SP3 until the first half of 2008.

We’re eagerly awaiting the release of the newest Microsoft OS, Windows “Longhorn” Vista, that has currently reached RC2 (Release Candidate 2 - for those who don’t know, it’s the final stage before the final version of the project).
