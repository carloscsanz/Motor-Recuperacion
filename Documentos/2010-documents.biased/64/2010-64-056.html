Who Founded Puerto Rico?

By Lesley Barker, eHow Contributing Writer

Puerto Rico is an island in the Caribbean that belongs to the United States. Its citizens are U.S. citizens. Even though the island was originally inhabited by Arawak Indians, this tribe was mostly wiped out by European diseases after it was colonized by Spain beginning in 1508 A.D. The conqueror and first governor of Puerto Rico was Juan Ponce de Leon.

    Identification

  1. Juan Ponce de Leon was born sometime near 1460 A.D. in Spain. No one is certain of the exact year of his birth. Later, he worked at the court of King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella, who were Christopher Columbus' patrons. The year of Columbus' first voyage, 1492 A.D., found Ponce de Leon fighting to drive the Moors out of Spain at Grenada. A year later, Ponce de Leon joined Columbus' second voyage to the new world.

    2. History

  2. After the ship landed on the island of Hispaniola, the home of both Haiti and the Dominican Republic, Ponce de Leon was involved in another military mission. This time it was to lead a group of Spanish soldiers to fight against the native people. He beat them at Higuey. Next, he was named the governor of this part of Hispaniola.

    3. Function

  3. In 1508, Ponce de Leon went exploring and discovered the island of Puerto Rico, where he also found gold. It only took one year for his soldiers to conquer the whole island. Then he became its first governor beginning in 1509 A.D. In 1512, he returned to Spain to tell the King about his conquests.

    4. Significance

  4. Ponce de Leon was very interested in finding the legendary Fountain of Youth, which many Europeans believed was at the site of the Garden of Eden on an island called Bimini. Ponce de Leon was convinced that this mythical place was in the Caribbean and he managed to get the king to order him to find and conquer it.

    5. Effects

  5. On Easter Day in 1513, Ponce de Leon landed on the Florida peninsula for the first time and gave it its name. He thought this would be where the Fountain of Youth would be found. He explored much of the western side of Florida looking for it, unsuccessfully. Next, he sailed to the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico and decided that the Fountain of Youth was there, somewhere. He went back to Spain and returned again to colonize Florida and Bimini, if he could ever find it, by the order of King Ferdinand. He was also supposed to conquer all the Carib Indians, which Spain feared because they were cannibals.

    6. Time Frame

  6. Six years later, after fighting with the Carib Indians, Ponce de Leon left Puerto Rico to start a new Spanish colony in Florida. The Florida Native Americans did not want the Spaniards to do that, so they resisted them. Even though 200 Spaniards traveled with de Leon, only a few of them survived the attack. The Spaniards fled on their ships to Cuba, 90 miles away. Ponce de Leon, the founder of Puerto Rico, had been shot with an arrow and he never recovered. After they arrived at Cuba, Ponce de Leon died.
