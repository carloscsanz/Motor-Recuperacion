Way back in August 2002 we took an apple and we turned it into the hideous prune-face you see here. We like to call him "Bob"

Well Bob got a bit lonely in the office, so we thought we make him a friend. Find out how to make your own shrunken apple head.

What You Need :
- An apple
- Apple peeler/knife
- A bowl
- Salt

What To Do :


1) Peel the skin of the apple and carve a face in one side
2) Place the apple in a bowl of super salty water (half a cup of salt in 4 cups of water stirred till all the salt has dissolved) and leave it for 24 hours.
3) Remove the apple and stand it in a warm, dry place for three to four weeks.

The idea is that your apple head shrinks as it dries through a process called osmosis. Sadly, this time it didn't go to plan.

Instead of drying out and shrivelling up, our new apple head, "Corey", went mouldy. In fact, Corey was a bit of a health hazard in the Rollercoaster team office!


Whats Going On:
What's going on and why did it go wrong?

We sought the advice of Dr Julian Cox, who is an expert on microbes in foods.

He said: "The room in which we left Corey may have had too much moisture in the air.

Even though the salt takes a lot of moisture out of the apple, the salt that's left will attract more moisture from the air, if it's there.

If enough moisture is attracted back to the apple, then it's wet enough for moulds to grow.

While it's nice that Corey started growing hair and a beard, just like me, it may not be too healthy for everyone around him."
