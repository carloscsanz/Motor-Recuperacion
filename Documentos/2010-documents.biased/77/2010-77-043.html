The Extended Boolean Model
Ever wonder about term weight models involving AND and OR Boolean queries? Feel free to discuss these here.


First, let's mention the limitations of conventional Boolean models. These models assume that


1. all terms in a query are equally weighted; thus, there is no need or provision for assigning a degree of importance to terms in a query.

2. for OR (ANY) searches, documents containing at least one of the query terms are as relevant as documents containing all of the query terms.

3. for AND (FINDALL) searches, documents not containing at least one of the query terms are as irrelevant as documents containing none of the query terms.


To overcome these limitations, The Extended Boolean Model has been proposed. The model is the doctoral thesis of Professor Edward A. Fox. A week ago, Prof Fox graciously sent me a lot of references on his pioneering work in this area, which I use for an article on the subject. Here is just a sample of the references he sent me.


E. Fox. Extending the Boolean and Vector Space Models of Information Retrieval with P-Norm Queries and Multiple Concept Types. Cornell University dissertation, Aug. 1983. Available from: University Microfilms International, Ann Arbor, Michigan.

G. Salton, E. Fox, and E. Voorhees. Advanced Feedback Methods in Information Retrieval. JASIS, 1985, 36(3): 200-210.

G. Salton, E. Voorhees, and E. Fox. A Comparison of Two Methods for Boolean Query Relevance Feedback. IP&M, 1984, 20(5-6): 637-651.

G. Salton, C. Buckley, and E. Fox. Automatic Query Formulations in Information Retrieval. JASIS, 1983, 34(4): 262-280.

E. Fox and M. Koll. Practical Enhanced Boolean Retrieval: Experiences with the SMART and SIRE Systems. IP&M, 24(3): 257-267, 1988.

E. Fox, S. Betrabet, M. Koushik, and W. Lee. Extended Boolean Models. In Information Retrieval: Data Structures & Algorithms, eds. W. Frakes & R. Baeza-Yates, Prentice-Hall, 1992, 393-418. Also on CD-ROM published by Dr. Dobb's Journal.


I've written a step-by-step article on The Extended Boolean Model Feel free to ask questions or discuss the model, if you wish.

"Extended Boolean Information Retrieval" by Salton, Fox and Wu was presented in 1983 and covers the same sort of thing. Their "p-norm" model means that you can generate a more general query which allows for term weighting and boolean operator parameters that vary the "strictness" of the operator. You have more control over the recall and precision.

It's very expensive though, but it is much better than the standard boolean model which is completely inadequate for IR purposes as you pointed out. It doesn't allow for any ranking. The extended model answers the problem by weighting the strength of the boolean connectives.

People are working on all sorts of different methods to improve things. It's just a matter of making them all good enough to be compared to one another to find the most useful one. Ah well, our work is never finished!

For those interested, here is some meaning to the p-norm exponent

When p = infinity

1. The model reduces to a normal Boolean system where term weights are not included. In addition, a p value of infinity gives the MIN or MAX value of the document.

2. the AND operator represents a strict phrase assignment; i.e. if all of the query terms are not present, then disregard the document.

3. the OR operator implements a strict thesaurus; i.e., any term being present selects the document.


Moreover, when

p = 1, this is equivalent to the DOT Product as computed by the Vector Space Model; all terms are independent - AND's and OR's have no distinction.

p = 2, this is equivalent to a normalized Euclidean Distance.

p = 3, with AND's and OR's, more terms are more important than less terms.

Several succinct reviews of Salton, et al paper can be found at
http://ei.cs.vt.edu/~cs5604/f96/art-summs/SALT83.txt


Open source implementations are possible with MySQL, Lucene or Egothor, to mention a few.

The above shows how the Extended Boolean Model (EBM) relates to the Vector Space Model. But, how about LSI?

Thanks to one of -Berkeley- Prof Marti Hearst's grad students, Preslav Nakov, we can see how EBM can be used with LSI. This is demonstrated in his paper

Extended Boolean Operations in Latent Semantic Indexing Search

The Nakov writes

"The paper presents a method for the usage of Boolean expressions for information retrieval based on Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI). The basic binary Boolean expressions such as OR, AND and NOT(ANDNOT) and their combinations have been implemented. The proposed method adds a new functionality to the classic LSI method capabilities to process user queries typed in natural language (such as English, Bulgarian or Russian) used in the "intelligent" search engines. This gives the user the opportunity of combining not only distinct words or phrases but also whole texts (documents) using all kinds of Boolean expressions. An evaluation of the implementations has been performed using a text collection of religious and sacred texts."


And if you ever wonder how AND, OR and NOT function look like as contour plots, check this one out.

On a side note, if you wonder of a work on LSI/LSA and the Bible (Old and New Testament) or the book of Mormons (or the Egiptian Book of Dead or the Quran), check this one from Nakov:

Latent Semantic Analysis of Textual Data Interesting.
