PROVIDE YOUR EVENT FEEDBACK

by Chelsea Wyatt
Where else is it possible to meet some of the most famous hockey and sports stars in the nation, rubbing elbows with ESPN, and to fight hunger at the same time? Nowhere else than at the Joe & Debbie Sakic Celebrity Bash.

For the past nine years, Joe and Debbie Sakic have been giving Coloradoans the chance to meet nationally recognized sports stars at their End of the Summer Bash. Over 500 people gathered for their end of the 2006 summer event.

The evening was a chance for all of these sports-fans to meet a Colorado legend, Joe Sakic and his wife in an informal setting. It began with a VIP cocktail hour on the back verandah of the Inverness Hotel and Conference Center. Overlooking the golf grounds and the spraying fountains, it was hard not to relax. In this easy-going atmosphere, locals got to rub elbows with ESPN and 9News figureheads, sports stars, and the donors to the Food Bank of the Rockies.

Back inside, the party really got into swing with half of Opie Gone Bad. They are one of the most recognized local bans in the states, and the smooth sounds coming from the stage represented the comfort in the rest of the room. This is only one of many charity events that they perform at every year. In fact, they were putting on their own Film on the Rocks charity event the very next evening!

The Railbenders came on next and definitely got the party going. Guests really got to know Joe Sakic and several other sports and media figures amid a sea of chocolate fountains, amazing hors d'ouevres and wonderful company.

At the end of the evening, some great items were auctioned off for the Food Bank of the Rockies. Amazingly, the main room packed with even more people who seemed to be flowing in from everywhere. The patio emptied, and even some of the hotel staff joined in the fun.

Unfortunately, some of the auction items were stolen during their move. The Food Bank of the Rockies staff scrambled to find replacements for merchandise in just three short weeks, including two round-trip tickets on Frontier Airlines, a 2005 Steelers Super Bowl Football, and an autographed Joe Sakic Quebec Nordique jersey.

Last year the auction raised $25,000 and provided 112,500 meals to the Children's Nutrition Network. The sponsors of the event opened their hearts to find great replacements for the stolen memorabilia. Some of the amazing finds at the auction included: NHL jerseys, autographed memorabilia, plane tickets and much more!

Food Bank of the Rockies, a non-profit organization, distributed over 20.5 million pounds of food last year through member agencies! These agencies support more than 900 hunger-relief programs in northern Colorado and Wyoming that helped provide more than 15 millon meals to the needy. Food Bank of the Rockies' four key programs are: Fighting Hunger Feeding Hope, Reclamatino, Denver's Table and Children's Nutrition Network.
