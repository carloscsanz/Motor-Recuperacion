Throughout the year, the Chief Executives' Club of Boston brings some of the world's top business leaders to Boston to share their views on some of the challenges facing business today. The Club works in partnership with the Carroll School of Management at Boston College to foster management excellence, leadership and good corporate citizenship.

To watch videos of the Chief Executives Club speeches, click on the 'Read More' tag for each event.

NECN is proud to partner with the Club to bring you this series on NECN and online at NECN.com.

Steve Forbes pointed his finger at policymakers and federal officials for their roles creating the economic collapse and the recession of 2008-2009.

STEVE BALLMER, CEO, Microsoft


On October 16, Microsoft Chief Executive Officer Steve Ballmer spoke to the Chief Executives Club of Boston.

JIM SKINNER, CEO, McDonald's


On October 27, McDonald's Chief Executive Officer Jim Skinner spoke to the Chief Executives Club of Boston.

Skinner has orchestrated the recent growth spurt of the company, and told the audience he continues to look at the regional differences in what works for McDonald's worldwide, and is looking to strengthen the company's perception as a fast food corporation that cares about healthy food.

ROBERT GREIFELD, CEO, NASDAQ


On September 22, NASDAQ Chief Executive Officer Robert Greifeld spoke to the gathered crowd at the Chief Executives Club of Boston.

Greifeld appeared relatively bullish on the economy, telling those on hand that the five upcoming IPOs that week, including that of Watertown, Mass., battery maker A123, suggested that there was an undercurrent of innovation in a choppy market.

TOM MENINO, Mayor, City of Boston


On June 9, 2009, the Chief Executives' Club of Boston welcomed a man who knows the city well. Mayor Thomas Menino has been mayor of Boston since 1993.
