Find How Many Pages of Your Site has Google Indexed?



Recently, many people some of them being my friends have asked me whether there is a way to find out the number of pages Google has indexed on their blog? Therefore; I decided to answer this question here, so that others may also take benefit of it.

Although, you may find a number of sites over the internet, such a Website Grader; being one of my top favorites, as it rates your site by evaluating certain parameters such as, Inbound Links, Page Rank, Technorati and Alexa Rank and especially Google Index Rate.

You may also follow the chart available inside Google Webmaster Tool, by going to Statistics -> Crawl stats. The First graph shows you how much google has indexed your site.

Besides, you can enter the following address shown below, inside the Google Search box.

site: www.DomainName.com

where DomainName will be the domain name of your blog/site. Once you hit the enter button you will see on the right the exact figure of Google Indexing.

All these methods will help you not only to determine how many pages your own site have, but you can also check to see how many pages your competitors also have in the Google search engine index.

The reason that you are seeing different numbers from Google is because you are looking for 2 different pages. One page is located on the sub-domain (www.) while the other is not. As far as Google would be concerned, you have at least 2 pages with duplicate content. If you have a /index.php or /default.asp (depending on programming language, etc.), then you have an even bigger problem. You should setup a 301 for all of the additional pages so that they go to the http://www.yoursite.com page. This will also redirect any linking that may have been done to these other pages so that it is focused on 1 page as opposed to 2 or 4 others. OK – I could go on for hours, so I will just finish by saying that the “site:” thing does not necessarily show accurate numbers…if you want more accurate info use their webmaster tools.
