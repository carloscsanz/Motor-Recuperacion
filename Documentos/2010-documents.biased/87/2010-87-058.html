Targus DEFCON CL Notebook Computer Cable Lock
CONS: I paid $30 for a lock that doesn't unlock. I read the instructions before I started playing with the lock, and of course I followed the instructions. I tested the lock outside of the computer and inside the computer, and then zero'd out the numbers before I left for school. At school I had to put the combo in because I zero'd out the numbers, and I locked my computer. After a few hours I tried to remove the lock, and it wouldn't work. I tried the default combo, my combo, and a couple hundred around both, then about a hundred random combo's.....about 500 total.

After searching online, I found that I was far from the only person to have this problem.....unfortunately the ratings on Newegg didn't alert me to this problem. It seems that these locks have a tendency to reset the combination on their own.

Targus doesn't have a telephone support phone number on their website until you use their website to alert them of a problem. I think that's a bunch of crap, so here's their phone number:

Monday through Friday from 7:00 AM - 4:00 PM PST at 877 4 TARGUS (877-482-7487).

I don't have time to wait on Targus to contact me, so I started looking for alternative ways of getting the lock off tonight. I found an article by Engadget where they were able to pick the lock. I tried their methods on my lock, but it wouldn't work on my lock...partly because of tighter tolerances, and partly because they've redesigned the lock again. I eventually Dremel'd the lock off.

PROS: It's small, easier to coil, and easy to lock.

OTHER THOUGHTS: I got the Targus combo lock so I wouldn't have to carry around another key. As I found out today, it's MUCH more convenient to have keys. I sent Newegg my story, and I hope to receive a refund. I've been a longtime customer, and this is my first problem, so hopefully they do something to help me out. I'm waiting to see what Targus will do. I think Targus is the one that should fix my problem, not Newegg, but I've heard Targus customer support is horrible. I love Newegg, and hate to send a claim their way, but I need that $35 to be productive. Hopefully Newegg learns a lesson replaces the troublesome Targus combo lock with something good.

Pictures:
I was hoping the Engadget lock picking trick would work, but the clearances are just too tight, even after I removed the plastic surround and the end of the lock.


My second attempt was to cut it off next to the swivel. It turns out that this would be exactly what Targus recommended in an email they sent me the next morning. Sorry for the blurriness from my 7 years old digital camera.
