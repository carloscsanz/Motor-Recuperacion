Pile Driving Relocation Work begins January 10

January 6, 2006

PORT MOODY The Port Moody Recreation Centre Expansion and Renovation project is well underway and pile driving relocation work will begin Tuesday, January 10, 2006 with completion expected in approximately two-and-a-half weeks.
 
The city is cautious with its investments and has been advised by the geotechnical engineer that some additional pile driving work is required to ensure that a stable base is constructed for the foundation of the facility.

The geotechnical engineers have advised that, Some piles require relocation and foundation enhancement to provide a solid support for the expansion of the recreation centre. This work is critical to provide the facility with a foundation that is stable and resilient to ground settlement, thereby avoiding costly repairs in the future.

Work will be carried out between 7:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m. Monday to Saturday (excluding holidays) in accordance with City of Port Moody Noise Bylaw #1399.

Port Moodys recreation centre expansion and renovation project includes a new Olympic-size ice sheet, gymnasium, fitness studio, spa, outdoor field house and bleachers and upgrades to the aging facilitys lobby and administrative areas. A $2 million Canada/British Columbia Infrastructure Grant was awarded in January for the arena size upgrade. The project is expected to be complete in early 2007.
