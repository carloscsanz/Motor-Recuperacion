Opposable Thumbs

Spike TV's Video Game Awards winners announced

By Ben Kuchera | Last updated December 11, 2006 1:53 PM

The Spike TV Video Game Awards winners have been announced ahead of the broadcast on December 13 at 10pm EST. The winners range from the expected—Oblivion took home Game of the Year—to the headscratchingly odd (if you forced me to sit down and pick a Best Song from a game, Yellowcard's track on the Burnout Revenge soundtrack would not have been the first song that popped into my mind). Twilight Princess took the Critic's Choice award while Gears of War took Best Multiplayer Game, Best Graphics, and Best Shooter. The Nintendo Wii received the Breakthrough Technology Award.

Getting back to some of the oddities, take a look at the Gamer's Choice: Character of the Year award. That's right, Jack Sparrow from the Pirates of the Caribbean game took the honors. It was nice having Johnny Depp's voice used in the game, but is that worth an award? Were the other characters this year that bland? I would have voted for Frank West from Dead Rising, myself. Also, did Burnout Revenge deserve to win best driving game two years in a row? It won for the PS2/Xbox version last year, and won again for the pretty-to-look-at—but rehashed—Xbox 360 version. Combine that with the Best Song award, and it's starting to smell like a fix.

The one reason to subject yourself to this broadcast is the possibility of seeing the opening cinematic from Burning Crusade, the upcoming World of Warcraft expansion... if they can keep it from being leaked ahead of time.

Does the video game industry even need an awards show? The winners this year are a long list of bland, safe choices, and awards shows are usually just people congratulating themselves on what a great job they've done with whatever it is they're winning an award for. Looking at the full list of awards, it seems as though the criteria consisted primarily of having a large marketing budget. Was Oblivion really the most addictive title from the past year? It was a great game, but wouldn't Lumines 2 or Every Extend Extra fit that criteria better? Or even Brain Age?

I would have liked to have seen more respect for smaller games and portable titles, and even games from some of the online services out there. Roboblitz was a great title, but with a small marketing budget and online releases only through Steam and Xbox Live Arcade, it fell under many people's radar, and that's a shame. The other puzzler is the fact that the PlayStation 3 is nowhere to be found. There's no love for Resistance: Fall of Man or any mention of the hardware at all. Also snubbed was Rockstar's Bully, which was one of the highest-rated games this year.  

A look at the previous years' shows shows the same story: the popular games win the day, and there are no surprises in the lists of winners for the last two years either. If the comments we've seen online are any indications, most hardcore gamers share our sentiments about the awards.

Perhaps the best thing about the Spike TV Video Game Awards is that Spike TV has the good graces to release the list of winners ahead of time, instead of making us watch the broadcast on December 13. With Samuel "Snakes on a Mother(*%#ing Plane!" Jackson hosting, you know you're in for a long night of awkward jokes from B-list celebrities pretending to care about video games.

Here's the list of the big winners this year: 

Game of the Year The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion (Bethesda Softworks)
Best Individual Sports Game Tony Hawk Project 8 (Activision/Neversoft)
Best Team Sports Game NBA2K7 (2K Sports/Visual Concepts)
Best Song "Lights and Sounds" by YellowCard in Burnout Revenge-XBOX 360 (Electronic Arts/Criterion Games)
Best Soundtrack Guitar Hero II (Red Octane/Harmonix)
Studio of the Year Epic Games (Gears of War)
Best Wireless Game SWAT Force (Vivendi/KAOLink)
Best Performance by a Human—Male Patrick Stewart in The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion (Bethesda Softworks)
Best Performance by a Human—Female Vida Guerra in Scarface: The World is Yours (Vivendi/Radical Entertainment)
Best Cast Family Guy (2K Games/High Voltage Software)
Most Addictive The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion (Bethesda Softworks)
Critic's Choice The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess (Nintendo/Nintendo EAD)
Best Driving Game Burnout Revenge-XBOX 360 (Electronic Arts/Criterion Games)
Best Action Game Dead Rising (Capcom)
