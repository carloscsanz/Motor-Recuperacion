self portrait...


courtesy of a newish app. This post from this blog led to me buying a camera app which I am currently putting through its paces.

A crispy blue day and the Sun was out hence the big black sunglasses. I thought it was about time I showed my face around here despite the tragic hair day/year.

(I'm trying to decide whether to keep on growing it or chop it all off. At the moment stuck in that horror somewhere- in- between land. I wish I could be like the Baby Chrissie doll my sister had as a girl. You could pull her hair out of a hole in the top of her head and voila! Long swingy hair. Press her belly button and the hair snapped back into her plastic head. Kind of scary and not much room for brains but at least you could switch between long and short depending on your mood when you woke up in the morning....)



 Grace enjoyed being able to play outdoors. The fact that she couldn't bend too much due to all the layers I made her wear, didn't seem to bother her one little bit.

Would it be too much to ask for a simple smile?

OK, enough happy snaps, time to get back to the rabbit....

Things that inspire me...


Yep, pencils.

Wool shops.

People.

This one particularly.

I've mentioned her before. I've told you that you must go and and take a look, spend a few minutes looking through her eyes and listening to what she has to say. Because what she does, she does simply, thoughtfully and with much grace and beauty.

Now she has a new website. 'a thousand cheering strangers'. A website about courage, grace and dignity, qualities which you can never have too much of.

Go ahead, take a look. Read the stories and leave a comment, a bit of encouragement.

Maybe even tell your own story.

Add your voice to the crowd of cheering strangers.

Go on.

Visit.

And then tell everyone you know to do the same. Help this very worthwhile website become everything it deserves to be.
