What Year Was American Airlines Founded?
American Airlines

American Airlines was founded in 1930 as American Airways after Aviation Corp. bought 82 small, independent air transportation services throughout the Midwest and the East in 1929. Avco formed American Airways to bring the air carriers under one umbrella to establish routes in New York, Chicago, Dallas, Boston and Los Angeles.

    Lindbergh Connection

  1.  
    A Stinson Trimotor Model A sits in front of an American Airways hangar in 1930.
     Charles Lindbergh's 1927 transatlantic flight sparked an investment frenzy in numerous airline operations, including Universal Aviation Corp. in St. Louis, which employed Lindbergh and was purchased by Avco.

    2. Beginning

  2.  
    Early logo of American Airways.
     Avco was created by Fairchild Aviation to finance the aircraft dealer Embry-Riddle Co., which ultimately led to buying other aircraft companies.

    3. Aircraft

  3.  
    The Ford trimotor aircraft.
     The first aircraft used by American Airways were the Universal Fokker 10A trimotor, the Ford trimotor, and later the Curtiss Condor biplanes and the Douglas DC-3.

    4. Too Many Routes

  4.  
    The Douglas DC-3 served as the flagship aircraft of American Airways.
     Each acquired airline operated its own passenger, freight or airmail route and ancillary services, creating a complex, if not chaotic, nationwide system of flight routes and schedules.

    5. Simplified Operation

  5.  
    A 1930s advertisement for American Airways.
     Frederick Coburn was appointed president in 1930 and immediately simplified routes by dropping some, combining others and eliminating poor-revenue-generating operations such as flight schools, sales operations, and air taxis.

    6. Last Move

  6.  
    The Douglas DC-3 in 1934.
     LaMotte Cohu assumed leadership from Coburn in 1932 but was challenged by board of directors member E.L. Cord, a luxury automaker, who won control of the company and renamed it American Airlines in 1934.
