Re: Real time synchronization in C# Posted by Jonathan on 27 Nov 2007 at 1:33 AM
If you do a HEAD request, one of the headers you get back in the response should be a Last-Modified header. You can look at that and compare it to the last date/time you saw there, and if it's changed you know there was an update to the file.

I can't think of any way to get the server to push this information to you; you'd just have to poll it regularly. Unless WebDAV is just the protocol that something else is using, and you can get the something else to notify you. (For example, Subversion runs over WebDAV, and you can set up commit time hooks).

Jonathan
