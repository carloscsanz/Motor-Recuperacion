Apple chief Steve Jobs has liver transplant

Steve Jobs, who has been on medical leave from his role as chief executive of Apple since January, reportedly received a liver transplant about two months ago.

 

By Rupert Neate
Published: 7:39PM BST 20 Jun 2009

Mr Jobs, who previously survived pancreatic cancer, has been seeking treatment for a "hormonal imbalance" which has caused him to lose a considerable amount of weight.

Earlier this year Mr Jobs said the hormonal imbalance would be "relatively simple and straightforward" to treat. But about a week later, he said the problem was more complex than he initially thought, and told employees he would be taking a medical leave of absence.

Mr Jobs, 54, is understood to have undergone the transplant operation at a hospital in Tennessee. The operation is believed to have been a success and Mr Jobs is expected to return to work later this month, although he may work part-time initially.

Tim Cook, Apple's chief operating officer, who has taken over the day-to-day running of the company in Mr Jobs's absence, is believed to be about to take up a more encompassing role and may be appointed to Apple's board. A spokesman for Apple declined to comment. Apple's share price, which has yo-yoed on previous speculation about Mr Jobs's health, rose 2.6pc to $139.48 on Friday.

Mr Jobs, who co-founded Apple in 1976, has been intimately involved with the creation of many of Apple's most popular products, including the iPhone.

A new souped-up version of iPhone went on sale on Friday. More than 40,000 iPhone 3GS devices are expected to be sold in the UK this weekend. It is available at Carphone Warehouse, Apple stores and from O2, the mobile phone operator with exclusive UK rights to the phone.
