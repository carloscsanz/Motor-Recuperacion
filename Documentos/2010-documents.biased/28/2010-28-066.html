Google Caffeine's Archives

posted in 24 Aug, 2009 by Manish Pandey  

Advantage: Google Caffeine

Trust Google to come up with really spectacular and exciting stuff each time. Right from its days of Google Search, every single application at Google has been revolutionary. Google, the original search giant decided to do something better in what it is best known for - Search. So what can be “Better than the Best?” The answer came in the form of Google Caffeine. Google Caffeine is available at this URL, for you all to try out.

With the growth in competition for search engines (Bing) Google’s new strategy was to better its search engine. Google Caffeine was the result of that. Google defines this project as “A Next-Generation Architecture for Google’s Web Search” on their blog on 10th august 2009.

As the Google blog puts it:

To build a great web search engine, you need to:

  • Crawl a large chunk of the web.
  • Index the resulting pages and compute how reputable those pages are.
  • Rank and return the most relevant pages for users’ queries as quickly as possible.

Although looking at this statement you might wonder, whats so different from the earlier Google Search we had? The answer is ‘Much Much More’.

Google Search has been recently challenged on four things by competitors Microsoft’s Bing, Twitter, Yahoo and Facebook:

  1. Real Time Search.
  2. Microsoft’s Bing proving to be something of almost an equal level to Google Search.
  3. Partnership between Yahoo and Microsoft on Search.
  4. Facebook’s interest in Real Time Search.

Google Caffeine is an attempt in the right direction by Google to answer to these challenges.

But the prime question about Google Caffeine is… Is it Better than the earlier search?

Before I answer that. Lets take a look at the points which make the Current Google Search great.

  1. Accuracy - This was the most brilliant thing about Google. Even when it indexed only a few thousand or even million pages, its search results were accurate and obviously much better than the search results given by the search engines of those times.
  2. Speed - With every result, Google showed you the time it had taken to come up with it. Now that was something really cool. There was absolutely no need for Google to display that, as people did not have anything to do with it. But this was a brilliant strategy on Google’s part, when initially it was trying to make its mark as a search engine. Speed impresses people when they are trying something like search and the when they saw the total time taken, it only added to the confidence people were starting to have in Google.
  3. Total Web Pages Indexed - More the number of web pages indexed, more are the number of web pages returned for a particular query. And more is the number of web pages returned, easy it is for the user to find what he is looking for. And the quicker he finds out, the more satisfied he leaves from Google and more are his chances to come back to Google to search something else. Google initially indexed few thousands of pages, something which has grown phenomenally in the past years with Google now indexing more than 8 Billion Web Pages. No other website comes in comparison to the number of web pages indexed by Google.

But if there was one thing which Google didn’t address (it would be wrong to say that they failed in this) was real time search. This is what Twitter has brought up. With real time results Twitter is best for searching the current events or breaking news. Google quickly added that feature in their web results to come up with something like this:

So lets take a look at the new Google Search Engine and compare it with the current one in terms of:

  1. Total web pages indexed
  2. Speed
  3. Accuracy
  4. Real time?

There you go, the time difference did reduce over there. It also did for some more complex queries, but again a great update in this new Google.

3. Accuracy:

Hmmm! So how do you test the accuracy of something, which is known for its accuracy.

Simple search results like “India” or even “Twitter” did not reveal much although I did find out that there is a slight toggling in the search results for “Google News” results, “Google Pictures” results and “You Tube” results.

Nothing great!!!

Again, although the number of web pages returned and the time taken were impressive enough with Caffeine, I didnt find much difference in the output of results.

Simply toggling of the web pages is what I would call it.

4. Real Time:

Real time is the current hot topic among internet users. Twitter started all this. With a simple question “What are you doing?” Twitter single handedly put real time search on the map when it started indexing its tweets.

Almost immediately Google put an “Show Options” link on its search result page, giving the users option to look at queries that were old by some hours, days or months.

But when talking about Google Caffeine you can see the “Real Time” results embedded into the heart of the algorithm.

Clearly Google Caffeine is much better than the current Google Search when it comes to displaying real time results like breaking news and current event.

A good thing in the right direction makes this new tweak victorious in Google Caffeine.

Conclusion:

So overall, Google Caffeine is a much better product than the current Google Search. Keeping the latest trends in internet and competition in mind, Google has come up with a better product and its certainly posing as a challenge to Bing, Twitter and Facebook.

Google’s Caffeine Sandbox pulled out temporarily

The first thing that comes into one’s mind when he thinks he is uncertain of a certain issue is to go online and search on Google. Well, Google is improving with developments in technology to provide us with something better called Caffeine Sandbox. But, Google is making small datacenter upgrades and as a result of this they are temporarily putting off the caffeine sandbox. So if you are in the process of trying to compare Google and Google Caffeine, patience is requested.

With Google improvising on itself by providing the Caffeine sandbox, the improvement in the speed and efficiency is what is targeted and they have indeed achieved this. With several different tools available you could view the changes and compare the actual Google retrieval with Google Caffeine Sandbox.

The face search caffeine compare is one such tool that was brought forward which helped used compares both these search pages in the same page but on different frames. Having the search results side by side in a single page will help the user understand how different the search process is in both these.

Another tool which was provided was the Google Caffeine bookmarklet which enables the user to view the same Google search in a Google Caffeine sandbox environment if just clicked upon.

With Google constantly trying to improve the conditions provided to the user this is indeed a landmark as they are developing something that is out of the normal. The original Google when invented was supposed to be just a search engine where now it is stretched to perform almost any operation like Gmail, Orkut etc. As technology progresses better environments are developed for the end user.

But this Caffeine Sandbox has been pulled out of plug temporarily. This was news that was announced on Twitter by Matt Cutts with an assurance that it will be back soon.

Now Compare Google and Google Caffeine with Two New Tools

Since Google Caffeine has been launched, there are several sources of information which gives details about what is different between this and the normal Google that we use. As the saying goes, ‘seeing is believing’, the best way to believe what exceptional results are produced, users can compare the performance of both these by themselves. Though it is just a two days since we saw the launch of Google Caffeine, quite a few tools are available that will help you in this process of comparison.

One of the tools is called the Facesearch Caffeine Compare. This is incredibly convincing as it provides the user with the search results provided by both Caffeine and regular Google next to each other in frames. This is one tool that will provide all the adequate evidence of the differences that exist and is real time. You don’t have to switch between tabs to just compare these two and find the differences.

Yet another tool that is quite handy in this aspect is the Google Caffeine Bookmarklet. Found on the Google Tutor blog, this is a simple to handle tool. Once you perform a search on Google.com, all you have to do is click this bookmarklet and it shows you the results of the same search in the Caffeine sandbox. Quite easy to make use of, this tool is gaining enough popularity.

Use these tools and make sure that you find out the prominent differences between these two versions. Not only will this help you understand the differences but will also prove to you the efficiency results too.

Late this afternoon another announcement was made that Google Caffeine is to be out for a few days. This is not something that you need to worry about because you can be sure that Caffeine will be back with the bang with all the necessary updates being performed.
