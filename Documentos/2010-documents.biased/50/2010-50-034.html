Introduction

As players ascend through the ranks in
Battlefield 2
they will gain the ability to
unlock
certain weapons. Each time a player is promoted to an eligible rank, they are given the opportunity to unlock one of seven unlockable weapons, one for each class, which they may subsequently use in place of the standard weapon for the given class. Players with the
Special Forces
expansion are granted an additional seven unlockable weapons. Ranks and unlockables apply to all servers which allow them, but only "official" (ranked) servers record and send back stats.

Tips & Tricks


Contact Grenades
Don't be afraid to shoot these at almost everything:
Versus Transports -


  • Buggy
    (one hit) (aim for ground under car for best results)

  • Open Jeep
    (one or two hits) (usually one)

  • Van
    (two or three hits) (usually two)

  • Closed Jeep
    (one or two hits) (usually two)

  • Boat (
    one hit) (aim for inside for best results)

  • Chopper
    (five hits plus) (aim for large open sides to take out crew members)

Versus Combat Vehicles -


  • APC
    (four hits plus) (aim for back hatch for best results)

  • Tank
    (five hits plus) (aim for back hatch and/or rotating assembly on top for best results)

  • Mobile AA
    (four hits plus) (aim for back hatch and/or rotating assembly on top for best results)

  • Chopper
    (four hits plus) (aim for windshield to take out pilot/gunner)

Sniper Rifle

  • When sniping standing up, you have a higher amount of recoil, so aim accordingly by how far you are from your target.
  • When sniping in the prone position your recoil amount goes down, so at around 120 yards, aim for the neck to get a headshot.
  • The closer you are to your target, the better your chances are of hitting him - so get close. No more than 200 yards.
  • If your fighting close range as a sniper, crouch, take one shot, then run towords him and finish him off with your pistol.
  • When sniping on top of a building, lay claymores right infront of the ladders, but dont lay close to them, they are very sensitive.
