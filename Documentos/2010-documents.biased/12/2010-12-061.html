Notepad + + 5.6.7 – Free editor for programmers

Notepad + + is a free editor that is intended mainly for programmers. He is known for the support of many programming and mark-up languages, including Visual Basic, C, C + +, C #, Java, PHP, HTML and XML.

His Features include the simultaneous processing of multiple files, custom syntax highlighting and the creation of bookmarks to easily find a code section at a later point in time. Unlike the built-in Windows Notepad, Notepad + + also controls the display and conversion of compiled under Linux and Mac OS documents.

One of the strengths of the program is the toolbox called Text “, which will automate and facilitate the replacement text. This allows for some character (double quote, ampersand, etc.) Insert escape slashes and remove, convert lowercase letters to uppercase, reversing words and even convert numbers to different formats.

We also have liked the ability to create macros and to extend the software through plug-ins. One of the criticisms of the incomplete and partly something unintelligible translation into German.

  • Enlarge recognition of clickable link from “http://” to any URI.
  • Fix the bug while changing the language list in Style Configuration; the “Save and close” button is enabled.

Google donates $ 2 million to Wikipedia

The Wikimedia Foundation will use the additional funds to purchase new infrastructures, such as improved bandwidth and a larger number of servers and routes to improve service given to users worldwide.
Google has donated $ 2 million to the non-profit foundation Wikimedia, which among other things is responsible for managing the free online encyclopedia and world’s largest, Wikipedia.
This amount will be used to improve the technological infrastructure of the service, which ultimately was not enough donations to offer the best conditions. Thus the foundation will purchase new servers and routers, and also hire more bandwidth, which will support the growing global traffic that supports Wikipedia.
Wikimedia has also indicated that some of that money will go to increase their efforts to make Wikipedia a more accessible and easy to use, all based on the relationship between Google and the Foundation.
The donation is the first that Google has made to Wikimedia and Twitter had confirmation from the founder of this non-profit foundation, Jimmy Wales.
The Wikipedia currently has over 14 million articles in hundreds of languages.

Windows 7: Microsoft improves its system for detecting pirated copies

Microsoft will make an update to its Windows Activation Tool Technologies (WAT), which verifies the authenticity of licensing Windows 7 installed on the PC.

Microsoft steps up its fight against software piracy by protecting more Windows7.

The Redmond company and will update its Windows Device Activation Technologies (WAT), which took last year the estate of the Windows GenuineAdvantage, and to verify the authenticity of licensing Windows 7 installed on computers .

From 16 February, when the next update, the WAT tool can detect the 70 stolen devices designed to hijack Windows 7 and install pirated copies of the operating system.

This update anti-piracy is not mandatory: it will be available for download next week and will appear in the Windows Update later this month via an update described as “important”.

If the tool Enabling Technologies Windows detects a pirated copy of Windows on a PC, the screen will turn black.

A watermark White will then appeared on the screen to indicate that the license used is not authentic. His name will finally show a series of warning messages indicating that it is using a pirated copy of Windows 7.

Recall that, in the words ofMicrosoft, 60 million licenses of Windows 7 have been passed since the launch of the OS last October.
