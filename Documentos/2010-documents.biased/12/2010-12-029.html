Why use WMA - WMA Vs MP3

New WMA reduce audio file sizes by 50% when compared to the same file encoded as MP3. While WMA does use a 64kb rate, the files sound virtually identical to 128kb MP3 files. Somehow Microsoft squeezes more quality from the lower bitrate. This is a very important feature, given the limited storage of palm-size computers.

On the other hand MP3 has become a real universal audio standard. People with a large selection of MP3 files will be annoyed to convert them over to WMA. MP3 sounds great and all your friends use it, so why mess with a good thing?

What format you use comes down to how badly you need the smaller file size of the WMA files. Don't care about small files? Stick with MP3. Want to cram as much high quality audio as possible into your palm-size computer?
