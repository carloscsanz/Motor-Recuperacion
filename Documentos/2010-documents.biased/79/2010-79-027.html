Facebook page launched

Derbyshire Constabulary has launched a page on the Facebook website.
 
Facebook is a social networking site that allows members to update friends about their current activities and enables them to join networks based on location, organisations or interests.

  

The force's Facebook page provides an overview of Derbyshire Constabulary and, utilising some of the unique features of the site, streams the most recent news releases, advises residents of ongoing campaigns and features the latest videos produced by the force. It also encourages members of the public to find out more about which Safer Neighbourhood team is policing their community and how they can contact them.
 
In addition to this, the page gives and insight into the work of Safer Neighbourhood teams. Bolsover Town Safer Neighbourhood Team is the first team to feature in this section. Other teams will appear in the future.
 
Ben Sherwin, Web Manager for Derbyshire Constabulary, said: "We're really pleased to establish our presence on Facebook, which is an increasingly active site. This will hopefully allow people who would not normally view our website to find out more about the force and the officers who patrol their community.
 
"We welcome any feedback the public has about the page and how we can improve it even further."
 
To view the site, visit www.facebook.com and search for Derbyshire Constabulary.
 
The launch of the force's Facebook page comes shortly after the force launched its own Twitter news service, which currently has more than 140 followers receiving news updates direct to their Twitter feeds.
