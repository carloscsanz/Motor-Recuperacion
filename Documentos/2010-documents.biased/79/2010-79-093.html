The past, present and future

The R n S Motorsports team would not be here today if it were not for the owners, Richard and Skeeter Storkson. The owners are very knowledgeable in the sport of auto racing and have over twenty years of experience in this demanding sport. The founder, Richard Storkson, served as a mechanic for a time before starting his racing career in 1987. Richard continued racing for eight years, with many more wins. More important, Richard gained the patience, knowledge and strength needed to build a winning racing team.

Meanwhile, Richard’s oldest son, Mike, a strong-willed little boy, watched from the stands of the track. Watching the drivers each week, he waited for the day, that he too may have his time in the winner’s circle. Growing up around the race track, Mike learned at a young age that the only way to really watch a race is from the driver’s seat, through the rearview mirror.  Now it is time to pass the keys from father to son. Mike not only has his passion for racing, but also a solid foundation which has and will support him, and allow him to continue to succeed in racing.

In 2004, Mike “The Spike” Storkson, started his racing career. Spike immediately showed his natural talent by winning the Bandit feature his first night of racing at Columbus 151 Speedway. Spike went on to win 50% of the heat races he participated in. Spike’s consistency paid off, never finishing a race out of the top ten, placing him, in his rookie season, ninth in points.  During this season the team set and fulfilled a number of goals.  These were to gain knowledge of car setups and performance, driver and crew experience, and to present a reliable team with great sportsmanship. We also earned the respect of sponsors, other race teams, owners, and track personnel for our conduct on and off the track.

In 2005, Spike moved into a higher, more challenging class, where he would be driving a 1977 Chevy Monte Carlo.  This family owned, WJJO sponsored, # 94.1 Solid Rock Hobby Stock was a welcomed challenge to the team whose persistence and hard work made this transition an easy one. Spike timed in 12 times in the top ten, three times in the top five, and as the fastest car at the track once.  In all his races for the year he finished 31 times in the top ten, 22 times in the top five and won three of his races. Overall Spike finished seventh in points, just a few points shy of “Rookie of the Year” at Columbus 151 speedway.  Spike did win the Summer Rumble for the Hobby Stock division; the first rookie to ever earn the title of Summer Rumble Champion.

Although “Rookie” mistakes are inevitable, Spike made very few, and was even told multiple times by seasoned drivers that they would much rather race with him, as they respect and trust him. To finish out the 2005 season Spike participated in the National Short Track Championship in Rockford, IL; an event which brings some of the best drivers from around the Mid-West. The feature, an event with a 27 car field, proved to be a challenging 40 lap race. Despite this Spike finished in the top five.


 

With his rookie season out of the way, Spike was ready to challenge himself yet again.  In the 2006 season the #94.1 Solid Rock Hobby Stock raced on the newly installed 1/4 mile at Madison International Speedway.  Not only did Spike prove his natural talent, but he blew the competition away.  In his heat races Spike won one race, and in 14 weeks of racing never finished outside the top five, consistently qualifying him for all the feature events.  In the feature events Spike never finished outside the top four, and went on to win four of these events.  Spike started the season strong earning himself the third place spot in points during week one. After this Spike never looked back, continued winning and earning more points.  Half way through the season Spike participated in and won the Hobby Stock Shoot Out. With six weeks left in the season Spike moved into first place.  Although the competition was extremely skilled, and experienced beyond Spike’s years, he continued to race strong, and earned his sponsors, team and himself the title of 2006 Madison International Speedway Hobby Stock Track Champion.

Each year Madison International Speedway asks the track attendees to nominate the driver that they believe was the most fun to watch and support. After the votes were tallied, the results showed that Spike had won by a landslide, earning the title of 2006 Madison International Speedway’s favorite driver award.

The 2007 racing season was as exciting and Spike went right to work racing into every feature event at every track that he attended. Spike and the 94.1 Solid Rock Hobby Stock made a season long tour to all three local tracks. At Columbus 151 Speedway he raced 11 nights out of 17 weeks and still finished a solid 6th in the championship point standings. At Madison International Speedway he ran all four shows offered to the Hobby Stock class, and at Jefferson Speedway Spike ran 3 weeks. In the 2007 season Spike had 3 top 5 and 8 top 10 finishes, qualified 5th or better 6 times and has 4 total wins 2 which were feature events. As this was Spike’s last year in the very competitive Hobby Stock division we were happy to have a very productive and consistent year.

The range of talent in this class is astronomical, as it ranges from 20 year veterans, all the way down to what was a very good rookie of the year battle. This was a challenge not only Spike but the entire RnS Motorsports team. Spike was one of only 3 rookies to earn a win. The challenge before us was and still is the adjustment to twice the track, twice the horsepower, and twice the dynamic technology.

            2009 is fast approaching and promises to be one of the biggest in RnS Motorsports history with the amazing acquisition of ESPN 100.5 FM as a sponsor. In the off season we invested in a new car, new motor, and brought on new experienced crew. This all will help to make this season one where we announce our presence as a powerful force to be reckoned with at with at Madison International Speedway.
