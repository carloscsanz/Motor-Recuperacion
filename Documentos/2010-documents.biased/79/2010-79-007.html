The 7800GS isnt a high end card, you probably wont be able to run it at high settings on Battlefield 2142 with maximum AA. The 7800GS Agp standard performs about 25% slower than the 7800GT PCI-express that was released nearly 18 months ago. Its a good card but it wont be able to play new games maxed out seeing as how its 25% slower than the older 7800GT. There is faster 7800GS+ cards out there with 24 pipes (7900 series core) and 512mb ram but not worth the money to plough into an AGP system. A new Cpu will help but you will still be bottlenecked by your 7800GS.

Using a 7800GS in either a x4 or x8 Agp slot wont affect performance at all. The 7800GS bandwidth requirements dont exceed what a x4 Agp slot can deliver , in short there will be no difference in performance between both as the 7800GS doesnt saturate a x4 Agp bus. PCI-E cards have been making headway for 2 years now, thats a long time and cards like the 7800GS Agp are extremley overpriced considering the performance they give out compared to PCI-E gfx cards. I guess they knew people would still buy them despite the PCIE offering much more performance for a lesser amount of money. I believe its far cheaper in the long run to upgrade to the pcie platform rather than throw money at legacy AGP platforms were you get far far less performance for the price you pay.

Heres a quick example, a 7800GS standard 256mb AGP costs around �190, you can buy a 7950GT 512mb PCIE 24 pipe card for the same price. You are looking at a seriously massive perf lead the 7950GT has and all for approx same price. Even cards like the 7900GS 256mb PCI-E will outperform the 7800GS Agp significantly and for around �80 less. They arent worth the cash but of course some people dont want to upgrade their platform but it costs more in the long run with far less perf.
